Small Business and "Corporate" Law

I can help you establish and maintain an LLC, partnership, joint venture or corporation.

Nowadays, you can pretty much use a limited liability company (LLC) to handle all your business transactions. But don't just file Articles of Organization, get an EIN, and think that's all of it. You really need a proper Operating Agreement, regardless of whether your LLC is single owner (just you) or if you have "partners".

Important Considerations:

  • Transfer on Death (TOD): Whatever you set up, make sure it will easily pass TOD to your heirs.
  • Buy/Sell Mechanisms: Create straightforward mechanisms to buy/sell - in case "partners" dispute.
  • Business Growth: Make sure your paperwork allows for business growth (new and continued investment).
  • Documentation: Document all actions taken. Cleanup/fix the messes that happen. Keep a good "corporate" record book.
  • Tax & Accounting: You have to pay taxes and you should associate with a good bookkeeper and accountant.

Estate Planning

You should sit down and take stock of what you own and to who it will pass. Today!

I can help with wills, trusts (new and existing), powers of attorney, advance directives (living wills, healthcare powers of attorney, organ donation), and funeral instructions.

End of life planning is NOT complicated. Keep things simple and stupid.

Helpful Tools & Strategies:

  • TODs, PODs, and Survivorship: Use these arrangements to protect your legacy when you die.
  • Powers of Attorney & Trusts: Use business powers of attorney and/or a trust to ensure your affairs are managed according to your wishes while you're alive.
  • Help Your Heirs: Make it easy for them to skip probate and promptly access and manage what they will own. Avoid as much process and paperwork as possible.
  • Your Effort Required: You'll likely have to request and submit bank, insurance company, brokerage, etc. forms.

You Want a Lawyer To:

• Organize a simple plan
• Minimize future family exposure
• Push you to complete the process
• Make sure you are using current forms
• Create language about your unique concerns
• Check that everything is executed and recorded appropriately

Litigation Avoidance

⚠️ If you're headed-for or currently involved in any type of court proceedings, you should promptly change gears.

Nobody ever wins a court case except the lawyers.

That may seem crass, but it's the truth. Don't believe me? Go ask your friends about their past lawsuit experiences.

Practically Speaking, Ask Yourself:

  • What would constitute a win in my scenario?
  • What is my budget?
  • What are my odds?

Candid Thoughts About Legal Disputes:

  • No Loser Pays: There is no loser pays system in the United States. Your attorney fees will be your own problem (except in VERY rare situations).
  • Courts Collect Costs, Not Solve Problems: A primary reason is court cases take a LONG time. Frankly, justice delayed is justice denied.
  • Procedural, Not Merits: Whatever you want to fight about will likely be resolved procedurally, not based on the merits.
  • Bankruptcy Reality: Often, judgements go to bankruptcy.
  • City Hall: You can't fight city hall. Well, you can, but you better have great resolve and deep pockets.
  • Big Business: Big business wants your money, even if only $2.99 a month. The customers' interests really don't matter.
  • Intellectual Property: IP is only as valuable as the amount you're willing to spend to defend it.

My Policy: I generally will NOT go to court - except maybe once in a while for an eviction or to collect a debt. You probably shouldn't either.

However, if I can, I would like to help you strategize how to avoid lawsuits or exit litigation.
